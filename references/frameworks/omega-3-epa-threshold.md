# Omega-3 EPA Threshold

## The Protocol

Consume at least **1,000 mg (1 gram) of EPA per day**. Not total omega-3 — specifically **EPA (eicosapentaenoic acid)**.

## Why EPA Specifically

Omega-3 fatty acids come in three main forms:

- **EPA (eicosapentaenoic acid)** — the anti-inflammatory and mood-regulating component
- **DHA (docosahexaenoic acid)** — structural component of brain cell membranes
- **ALA (alpha-linolenic acid)** — plant-based omega-3, poorly converted to EPA/DHA (roughly 5–10%)

DHA is important for brain structure, neuronal membrane integrity, and infant brain development. But the research on **mood, depression, and systemic inflammation** points consistently to EPA as the active compound.

## The Evidence for 1,000 mg EPA

Multiple meta-analyses have shown that omega-3 supplementation with **EPA-dominant formulations at 1,000+ mg EPA/day** produces antidepressant effects comparable to or augmenting SSRIs:

- A 2019 meta-analysis in *Translational Psychiatry* found that EPA at ≥1 g/day had significant antidepressant effects
- The effect was EPA-dose-dependent — higher EPA relative to DHA = better mood outcomes
- Formulations where EPA comprised >60% of total omega-3 were most effective
- DHA-dominant formulations showed weaker or no antidepressant effects in the same analyses

Huberman has cited this threshold repeatedly: **1,000 mg EPA minimum** is where the mood and anti-inflammatory benefits become clinically meaningful.

## The Label Problem

Most fish oil capsules are deceptively labeled. A "1,000 mg fish oil" capsule typically contains:

- Total fish oil: 1,000 mg
- EPA: 200–300 mg
- DHA: 100–200 mg
- Other fats: 500–700 mg

This means you'd need **3–5 standard capsules per day** to hit 1,000 mg EPA.

**What to look for on the label:**

1. Find the "Supplement Facts" panel
2. Look at EPA specifically (not "total omega-3")
3. Calculate how many servings you need to reach 1,000 mg EPA
4. Higher-concentration formulas (500+ mg EPA per capsule) require fewer pills

## EPA's Mechanism of Action

EPA reduces inflammation and supports mood through several pathways:

1. **Competes with arachidonic acid (AA):** AA is an omega-6 fatty acid that produces pro-inflammatory eicosanoids (prostaglandins, thromboxanes). EPA competes for the same enzymatic pathways (COX and LOX enzymes), shifting the balance toward anti-inflammatory mediators.

2. **Produces resolvins and protectins:** EPA is converted into specialized pro-resolving mediators (SPMs) that actively resolve inflammation rather than just suppressing it.

3. **Modulates neuroinflammation:** In the brain, EPA crosses the blood-brain barrier and reduces microglial activation — the brain's inflammatory response that is implicated in depression.

4. **Supports serotonin function:** EPA may enhance serotonin release and receptor sensitivity, which connects its anti-inflammatory properties to its mood effects.

## Practical Options

### Fish Oil Capsules
- Most accessible, widely available
- Choose brands with third-party testing (IFOS, USP, NSF)
- Look for concentrated formulas (e.g., 500–750 mg EPA per capsule)
- Typical dose: 2–4 capsules per day
- Store in the fridge to slow oxidation and reduce fish burps

### Liquid Fish Oil
- Often more cost-effective for high doses
- Easier to hit 1,000 mg EPA in a single tablespoon
- Flavored versions (lemon, orange) mask the taste
- Huberman has mentioned liquid fish oil as an efficient delivery method

### Whole Fish
- Fatty fish 2–3x per week provides substantial EPA:
  - Salmon (3 oz): ~700–1,000 mg EPA
  - Mackerel (3 oz): ~500–900 mg EPA
  - Sardines (3 oz): ~400–700 mg EPA
  - Anchovies (3 oz): ~500–750 mg EPA
- This may be sufficient if fish intake is consistent, but many people supplement on non-fish days

### Algal Oil (Vegan)
- Derived from microalgae (the same source fish get their omega-3s from)
- Typically higher in DHA than EPA — harder to hit the EPA threshold
- Some brands offer EPA-enriched algal oil
- Usually requires higher volume than fish oil

## Take with Fat

Omega-3s are fat-soluble. **Take fish oil with a meal that contains dietary fat** for optimal absorption. Taking fish oil on an empty stomach or with a fat-free meal reduces bioavailability significantly.

## Caveats

- High-dose fish oil (>3 g total omega-3/day) can have blood-thinning effects. If you're on anticoagulants (warfarin, aspirin therapy), consult your physician.
- Fish oil quality varies enormously. Rancid fish oil may be pro-inflammatory. Buy from brands that publish their TOTOX (total oxidation) values.
- Mercury concern: reputable fish oil brands distill out heavy metals. Whole fish (especially large predatory fish like tuna and swordfish) carry more mercury risk than fish oil supplements.
- The antidepressant evidence for EPA is strongest as an *adjunct* to standard treatment, not as a standalone replacement for psychiatric medication.
